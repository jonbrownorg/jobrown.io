Frequently Asked Questions

How does Animal Age calculate animal ages compared to humans?

Animal Age uses commonly accepted lifespan averages and age-conversion models for each species and breed. These models compare an animal’s age progression against a typical human lifespan to provide an easy-to-understand comparison rather than an exact biological equivalent.

Are the age comparisons scientifically exact?

No. The comparisons are intended to be informational and illustrative, not medical or veterinary advice. Lifespans can vary widely based on breed, genetics, environment, and care, so results should be viewed as general guidance.

Can I track my own pet’s age in the app?

Yes. Animal Age allows you to enter and track your pet’s age and basic information directly on your iPhone. This makes it easy to see how your pet’s age compares to human years over time.

Why do some animals have very different age conversions?

Different species — and even different breeds — age at very different rates. For example, smaller animals often mature faster and have shorter lifespans, while larger animals tend to age more slowly. Animal Age accounts for these differences when presenting comparisons.

Does Animal Age support multiple animal species?

Yes. In addition to common household pets, Animal Age includes a variety of species such as birds, livestock, and other animals. This allows you to explore age comparisons across a broad range of animals for learning and curiosity.
