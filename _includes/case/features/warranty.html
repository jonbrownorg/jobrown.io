Frequently Asked Questions

How does the app authenticate with Apple Business Manager?

ABM Warranty uses Apple’s official OAuth 2.0 client authentication flow. You generate an EC P-256 private key in Apple Business Manager and provide the required identifiers within the app. All credentials are stored locally on your Mac and are never transmitted to any third-party service.

Is this safe to use in large environments?

Yes. The app is designed and tested for large organizations with tens of thousands of devices. It includes pagination safeguards, serialized coverage requests, retry handling, and protections against malformed or partial ABM API responses to ensure stability during large imports.

Does the app modify any data in Apple Business Manager?

No. ABM Warranty is strictly read-only. It retrieves device inventory and warranty information from Apple Business Manager but does not create, update, or delete any data in your ABM tenant.

What happens if some devices fail to load or return errors?

Individual device failures do not stop the overall import. If Apple Business Manager returns errors for specific devices, the app continues processing the remaining inventory and records failures so you can review them later. This prevents partial API issues from disrupting large data pulls.

Can I export warranty and coverage data?

Yes. ABM Warranty allows you to export retrieved warranty and AppleCare+ coverage information to CSV format. This makes it easy to use the data for audits, lifecycle planning, budgeting, or integration with other internal tools.
